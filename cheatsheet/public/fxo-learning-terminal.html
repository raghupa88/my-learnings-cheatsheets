SALES — ROLE DEEP DIVE
Client Intake
Understand the client's FX exposure (e.g., a USD receivable in 3 months), hedge objective, risk tolerance, and premium budget.
Structure Design
Recommend appropriate strategy — vanilla, collar, seagull, risk reversal — based on budget and market view. Often iterative.
RFQ Submission
Enter all deal terms into Bloomberg OVML, ION Totem, Refinitiv Eikon, or internal platform. Send RFQ to trader's desk.
Client Quote
Add sales spread (3–15 bps typical for vanilla) to trader's mid-price. Quote in %, pips, or CCY amount — whatever client understands.
Execution
Verbal "done" captured on recorded line. Pass to operations for booking. Send deal term sheet to client for acknowledgement.
TRADER — ROLE DEEP DIVE
Vol Surface Check
Pull live vol surface for the currency pair. Check ATM, 25D Risk Reversal, 25D Butterfly for the relevant tenor.
Model Pricing
Run Garman-Kohlhagen for vanilla; local vol or stochastic vol (Heston, SABR) for exotics. Output: premium + Greeks.
Book Risk Check
Assess how this new trade affects aggregate book delta, gamma, vega, theta. Does it offset existing risk or add to it?
Bid/Ask Spread
Widen around mid based on liquidity, tenor, size, and exotic complexity. Vanilla 1M = 2–5 vega points; exotic = much wider.
Delta Hedge
Execute spot/forward hedge immediately after booking to neutralize delta. Re-hedge dynamically as spot moves (gamma cost).

Pricing Engine
How the trader prices a vanilla FX option using the Garman-Kohlhagen model — Black-Scholes adapted for two risk-free interest rates (one per currency).
GARMAN-KOHLHAGEN — MODEL INPUTS
INPUTSYMBOLDESCRIPTIONEXAMPLE
Spot RateSCurrent live market exchange rate (CCY1/CCY2)1.0842
Strike RateKAgreed exercise rate from the RFQ1.0800
Time to ExpiryTYears from today to expiry. Day count: Act/365 (or Act/Act)0.2500 (≈ 3M)
Implied VolatilityσMarket's forward-looking vol for this tenor & delta. Pulled from live vol surface — NOT historical vol.6.85% annualised
Domestic Rater_dRisk-free rate of CCY2 (quote currency). For EUR/USD: USD SOFR rate.5.25% (USD)
Foreign Rater_fRisk-free rate of CCY1 (base currency). For EUR/USD: EUR rate.3.75% (EUR)
G-K FORMULAE
CALL PRICE (right to buy CCY1)
C = S·e−r_f·T·N(d₁) − K·e−r_d·T·N(d₂)
PUT PRICE (right to sell CCY1)
P = K·e−r_d·T·N(−d₂) − S·e−r_f·T·N(−d₁)
d₁ AND d₂
d₁ = [ ln(S/K) + (r_d − r_f + σ²/2)·T ] / (σ·√T)

d₂ = d₁ − σ·√T
PUT-CALL PARITY (FX)
C − P = S·e−r_f·T − K·e−r_d·T
N(·) = cumulative normal distribution function.
The key difference from Black-Scholes: spot is discounted at r_f (CCY1 rate), not just risk-free rate — because holding the base currency earns the foreign interest rate.

TWO-LEG STRUCTURES
RISK REVERSAL
▲ DIRECTIONAL · NEAR ZERO COST
LEGS
Buy OTM Call (25D) + Sell OTM Put (25D)
MARKET VIEW
Spot rises. Willing to sacrifice downside participation to fund the upside hedge.
MAX LOSS (DOWN)
Obligated to buy CCY1 at put strike if spot falls hard (unlimited loss below put strike)
MAX GAIN
Unlimited above call strike
PREMIUM COST
Near zero — call premium funded by put premium received. Small net pay/receive.
KEY RISK
Short put = obligated if spot collapses. Client must understand this tail risk.
CLIENT USE CASE
Importer: buy USD Call (25D), sell USD Put (25D) — free upside protection, but locked if USD weakens
INTERBANK QUOTE
"1M 25D RR" = vol differential between 25D Call and 25D Put. Positive = calls richer.
ZERO-COST COLLAR
◆ HEDGE WITH FLOOR + CAP
LEGS
Buy OTM Put + Sell OTM Call (for exporter hedging CCY1 receivable)
MARKET VIEW
Accept a band of rates. Protect downside at a floor; give up upside above cap.
FLOOR
Put strike = worst-case rate for exporter. Always protected below this level.
CAP
Call strike = best-case rate. Upside capped here.
PREMIUM COST
Zero — put and call premiums sized to match. Very popular corporate hedge.
KEY RISK
Misses favourable spot moves beyond the cap. Client "gives up" potential gains.
CLIENT USE CASE
Exporter with EUR receivable: buy EUR Put floor at 1.0600, sell EUR Call cap at 1.1100. Free band protection.
EQUIVALENT TO
Risk Reversal — same economic structure, different naming convention by context

SEAGULL
▲ DIRECTIONAL · CAPPED · NEAR ZERO COST
LEGS
Buy OTM Call (25D) + Sell ATM Call + Sell OTM Put (25D)

Moderate spot rise expected (not runaway). Budget-conscious client.

If spot = ATM strike exactly at expiry (bell curve peak)

Bell-curve — earns maximum at ATM, loses premium beyond the wings

Negative — profits if implied vol falls after entry

Hedging the FXO Book
After selling an FX option, the trader does not take naked risk. Each Greek exposure is identified and hedged — using spot, forwards, and other options.
DELTA HEDGING
What it is
Neutralising directional spot exposure from the option. Delta = notional units of CCY1 the trader is effectively long or short due to holding the option.
Example
Trader sells EUR/USD Call 10M, delta = −0.50. Trader is effectively short 5M EUR. Hedge: buy 5M EUR spot.
Instrument
FX Spot or FX Forward (forward preferred for maturity-matching with the option delivery date)
Dynamic nature
Delta changes as spot moves (due to gamma). Trader re-hedges delta when it drifts beyond a threshold — called "gamma scalping."
Re-hedge trigger
Some desks re-hedge at fixed intervals (hourly), others at fixed delta shift thresholds (e.g., every 0.05 delta move).
Hedging cost
Each re-hedge costs bid/ask spread × lot size. High-gamma positions near expiry cost most to delta-hedge. This is "gamma bleed."
GAMMA HEDGING
What it is
Gamma = how fast delta changes per spot move. Short gamma = delta chases spot against you. Must re-hedge constantly (expensive).
Who has it
Option sellers are short gamma. Option buyers are long gamma. Short gamma = convex risk: large moves hurt regardless of direction.
How to hedge
Buy options of similar maturity and strike to offset short gamma. Cannot be hedged with spot or forwards — only options create gamma.
P&L from gamma
P&L ≈ ½ × Γ × (ΔSpot)². Long gamma: profits from large moves. Short gamma: loses on large moves in either direction.
Gamma vs Theta
Long gamma = earn from spot moves but pay theta (time decay) daily. Short gamma = earn theta but hurt by large spot moves. Implied vol prices this tradeoff.
VEGA HEDGING
What it is
Vega = P&L sensitivity to 1% change in implied vol. Short vega = hurt if market prices in more volatility after you sold the option.
Who has it
Option sellers are short vega (sold expensive vol; hope it falls). Option buyers are long vega (hope vol rises to inflate their option's value).
How to hedge
Buy/sell options of the same tenor to offset net book vega. Must match tenor — 3M and 1Y vol move independently (vol curve risk).
Vega bucketing
Traders run vega reports by tenor bucket (1W, 1M, 3M, 6M, 1Y, 2Y). Each bucket is hedged separately — cross-tenor hedges leave basis risk.
Realised vs Implied
If realised vol (actual spot moves) is lower than implied vol at trade entry, short vega wins. Daily theta earned > dynamic hedge cost. Core driver of vanilla option seller P&L.
